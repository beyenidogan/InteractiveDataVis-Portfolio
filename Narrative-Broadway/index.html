Broadway Theater
Broadway Theater is has a significant contribution to New York City's economy had one of its best years in 2019 with $1.83 billion in ticket sales and 14.8 million in total attendance.
Where exactly is Broadway?
The first theaters in New York City was opened in 1750 on Nassau Street with capacity of 280 for performances of Shakespearian plays and operatic theater. Later, beginning around 1850 the heart of theater moved from downtown gradually to midtown Manhattan due less expensive real estate at the time.

Today Broadway Theater refers to a collection of 41 venues with a seating capacity of at least 500 people, between West 40th and West 53rd Streets. Only four of them are actually on Broadway. If a facility hosts concerts and dance shows more often than it does plays or musicals, it isn't considered a Broadway theater, regardless of the seating situation.

Those with 100 to 499 seats are regarded as "Off-Broadway" venues regardless of the location.

Meanwhile, establishments with 99 seats or fewer are deemed "Off-Off-Broadway" which are more widely dispersed throughout the city.
